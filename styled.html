Introduction

Super Smash Bros is a series of fighting video games that features many characters from other established video game franchises. While the developer intended for it to be a fun party game, the community has created a different way to play the game to make it more competitive. Ledgetrapping is a technique used in more recent titles in the series that involves the player performing the technique "trapping" the opposing player at the edge of the stage by restricting their options of getting back. I will be focusing on the aspects of ledgetrapping found in the most recent installment, Super Smash Bros Ultimate (SSBU) for the Nintendo Switch. Advantages

Ledgetrapping provides many benefits when performed correctly and consistently. Being able to ledgetrap your opponnent can quickly change the momentum of the match.

The entire basis of ledgetrapping is the huge advantage of your position when compared to your opponnent. The ledge (also known as the edge) is any part of the map/stage that your character can grab onto. It is usually found at the end of the main platform. As a result, the edge is generally an important aspect in the battle between the person trying to recover back onto the stage and the other player who is trying to stop them. Because of the unique nature of KOing your opponent in this game, utilizing the ledge to knockout your opponent is a common strategy.

^The red part represents the ledge

When your opponent is on the ledge, their options decreases significantly. If they were already onstage, they could make use of all of the moves in their character's arsenal. However, when they are on the ledge they are limited to only six primary options and sometimes a character-specific option. These six options are:

  1. Neutral getup
  2. getup attack
  3. roll
  4. release
  5. stall
  6. jump

Knowing this, the player onstage has a huge advantage over the player on the ledge. This is because the player onstage has their entire moveset while the player on the ledge will only have a few options. The goal of the player onstage is prevent their opponent from getting back onstage. This is where the "trapping" part of ledgetrapping comes into play. By covering their potential ledge options, not only do you prevent them from returning to a neutral position, but you also slowly build up damage on your opponent. When done correctly, ledgetrapping can sometimes win you the game.

Techniques

Now the most important part. How to actually perform it! Now naturally, it is impossible to know which of the 6 options your opponent is going to choose everytime. Unless your opponent does the same thing time and time again, they will eventually escape your deadly trap. So how do you know which option your opponent will choose? Although it isn't humanely possible to guess correctly everytime, there are ways to make your life a lot easier and it all revolve around positioning. Basically, you can limit the number of options your opponent has from 6 to a more manageable amount like 2 or 3. You can do this by covering certain options and encourage your opponent to choose other ones. What do I mean by this? Here is an example. You can position your character to cover two options: Neutral getup and getup attack by simply holding shield right next to the ledge. If your opponent decides to pick either of these options, they will be serverely punished. Why? Because you are right there ready to hit them if they try to get back up. This doesn't directly take away your opponent's options, but it does discourage them from picking certain ones, narrowing down their decision.
